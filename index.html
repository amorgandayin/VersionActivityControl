Our Heritage

More than just a meal.

Filipino cuisine is a vibrant tapestry of Malay, Spanish, Chinese, and American influences. It is defined by the "linamnam" (deliciousness) and the balance of salty, sour, and sweet flavors.

At its core, our food is about Salo-Salo—the act of eating together. Whether it's a grand fiesta or a simple dinner at home, every dish is served with a side of warmth and hospitality.

Chicken Adobo

Chicken Adobo is considered the national dish of the Philippines. It is simmered in soy sauce, vinegar, garlic, bay leaves, and peppercorns to create a rich savory flavor.

Pork Sinigang

Pork Sinigang is a sour soup dish traditionally flavored with tamarind. It includes vegetables like kangkong, radish, eggplant, and tomatoes.

Crispy Lechon

Lechon is a whole roasted pig famous for its extremely crispy skin and juicy meat. It is the centerpiece of many Filipino celebrations and fiestas.
